The challenge of today's modern website

Creating a new website today takes a slight mental adjustment. We now have to think of all the different platforms your site might be viewed from. Once apon a time it was simply the humble desktop, now days your audience will more than likely be finding you from a phone or tablet device. So what does this mean? Take your current website for example, if it's been around for a few years, its likely to be a fixed width site that gets shrunk down when viewed on a mobile device. This means a lot of frustrating zoom in / zoom out behaviour for your viewers - far from ideal!

Our design process


Planning

This is the most critical phase of any website build. Here we lay the foundations of how your website will look, function and communicate. Time spent in this early planning stage mapping out key objectives, determining your target market and developing strategies for users to contact you will significantly impact the success of your website.

Designing

Here we develop the visual concepts of how your wesbite will look, including full mock ups of all the key template pages within your website. We focus on the layout of the key components (established in the planning stage), their positioning, styling, colour pallets, use of photography, typography and other design elements and features.

Coding

Upon reaching agreement on the design of your new website, we then convert your mock ups into HTML and CSS code allowing them to function within a web browser. Additional functionality such as special affects, forms, dynamic data derived from a database or a fully integrated CMS (content management system) are also implemented here.

Going Live!

Finally we test all functions and components of your site including forms, dynamic data delivery, content management, cross browser compatibilty, the responsiveness and appearance of your site on different devices such as smart phones and tablets. Once this has been completed we configure the hosting environment and launch your site live!

eLearn Economics


eLearn Economics - Online Learning

Overview ...

Technologies used: HTML5, CSS3 (Bootstrap), jQuery, PHP (Codeigniter framework), MySql database.

Scope: Rennie Resources Ltd originally published paper based economics resources for secondary schools in New Zealand. We were tasked with converting these resources into an online platform that would allow for immediate updates and additions, keeping content fresh and relevant. The site also allows for students to complete online tests and track their results through the year anabling them to monitor their progress and better prepare for exams.

Responsive: As the site is primarily focused on students it was important thay they could access and use it via mobile devices as well as a desktop environment in classrooms.

Athletics New Zealand


Athletics New Zealand - Results and Rankings

Overview ...

Technologies used: HTML5, CSS3 (Bootstrap), jQuery, PHP (Codeigniter framework), MySql database.

Scope: Design and build an online rankings and results database to replace the existing paper based PDF/word doc lists which were updated on an irregular basis. Site also required the facility to view full athlete profiles showing personal bests performances, yearly progressions, national representation and medals won at national championships.

Responsive: As this site contains a large amount of multi-column tabluar data we designed a solution that works effectively on smaller devices, especially phones, by initailly hiding less important data and then allowing the user to click and display the remaining information verticaly.
